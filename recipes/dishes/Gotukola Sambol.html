Gotukola Sambol

A green salad with chopped Gotu kola and freshly scraped coconut gives you one of the healthiest salads in Sri Lankan cuisine. Gotu kola is also known as Pennywort and can be added to any curry menu. This simple green salad idea is a vegetarian, vegan dish you can mix with your rice and curries to give you an instant wholesome meal.

prep time

20min

cook time

5 min

serving

3 servings

Tags : Dishes

instructions

step 1

Wash and clean Gotu kola.

step 2

Dry them off using a salad spinner. Or leave them in a colander to drain all the water out.

step 3

Put the leaves and stalks into a bundle and slice them thinly.

step 4

Make sure to use a sharp knife so you won't bruise the leaves. (You can cut them into thicker slices too)

step 5

Into a bowl, add coconut, onions, green chili, black pepper, salt, Maldives fish chips, and lime juice. Mix very well with your fingers (wear gloves).

step 6

Then add chopped Gotu kola leaves and tomato.

step 7

Mix gently but thoroughly without bruising the leaves. Adjust salt and lime to your taste.

ingredients

80 grams grated or shredded fresh coconut.

1⁄3 cup thinly sliced Pearl onions/Asian shallots/red onion (1/3 cup = 5 pearl onions)

1 green chili thinly sliced.

1⁄2 teaspoon coarse ground black pepper

1⁄2 teaspoon salt (adjust to your taste)

1 tablespoon Maldives fish chips (optional)

1⁄4 of a large lime for lime juice

80 grams Gotu kola leaves with stalks (around 2 cups when sliced)

1⁄2 of a tomato sliced or diced*

tools

Bowl

Knife
