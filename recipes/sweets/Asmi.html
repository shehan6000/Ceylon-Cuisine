Aasmi

A traditional Sri Lankan recipe, Aasmi is here to steal your hearts right away. This delicious and irresistible dessert snack recipe is the most popular sweet in Sri Lanka and is served in literally all their festivals and special occasions.

prep time

5 min.

cook time

10 min.

serving

8 servings

Tags : Sweets

instructions

step 1

Combine coconut milk and rice flour to make a stiff dough. To make this yummy recipe, sieve the rice flour through a fine sieve to filter any unwanted particles. In a medium-sized bowl, combine coconut milk, rice flour, and salt. Knead a smooth and stiff dough out of them.

step 2

Add cinnamon leaf extract to the dough and make a batter. Next, take cinnamon leaves (also called daul kurundu leaves) and grind them. Extract juice from the kurundu leaves and add to the coconut-rice flour dough. Stir until you get a slimy consistency. If you want, you can add more coconut milk to get a batter with perfect pouring consistency.

step 3

Pour the entire batter into oil to deep fry and keep aside for few days. Preheat refined oil in a deep bottomed frying pan on a medium flame. After the oil is hot enough, arch your fingers and push the entire batter into the hot oil and deep fry. After the batter is cooked through, transfer into a dish and cover it. Keep it aside for a few days.

step 4

Prepare the coloured thick sugar syrup for the topping of aasmee. After a few days, prepare a sugar syrup by simmering water with sugar and edible food colour. After you get a very thick consistency, keep it aside to cool.

step 5

Fry the aasmee again and design with coloured sugar syrup. Preheat refined oil in a deep bottomed frying pan and fry the aasmee again to make it light and crispy. Design the aasmee with the coloured thick sugar syrup by making beautiful designs and swirls. Serve fresh.

ingredients

500g of rice flour

4 pieces of cinammon

Refined oil as required

Salt as required

50g of sugar

2 drops of edible food colour

20ml of water

tools

A sieve

A medium sized bowl

A frying pan
