Watalappan

This Sri Lankan Watalappan is a classic! A delicious and creamy Baked Cardamom Spiced Coconut Custard and sweetened with unrefined jaggery or sugar!

prep time

15 min.

cook time

40 min.

serving

36 servings

Tags : Sweets

instructions

step 1

Preheat the oven to 160°C. Lightly grease a 23 cm square baking tray or dish with olive oil spray.

step 2

Combine the jaggery and 60 ml (¼ cup) water in a small saucepan and stir until it is fully melted with no lumps. Simmer for 1-2 minutes, then set aside and allow to cool.

step 3

In a large mixing bowl, combine the eggs, egg yolks and spices and whisk gently. You don't want to create too many air bubbles. Whisk in the coconut milk until well combined, then whisk in the cooled jaggery syrup. Pour the mixture into the prepared tray through a sieve - this will ensure the mixture is smooth and any lumps or impurities have been removed. Cover the tray tightly with foil. Place the tray in a bigger dish and fill the dish with hot water to about three quarters of the way up the side to create a water bath. Bake for about 45 -60 minutes or until the custard is set, but still slightly wobbly in the middle.

step 4

Remove from the oven and let the custard cool in the water bath for about 15 minutes. When cool enough to handle, remove from the water bath and cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 2-4 hours. It's best chilled overnight.

step 5

Slice into 8 slices and serve with some jaggery syrup if serving, and roasted cashew nuts sprinkled over the top.

ingredients

250g of kithul jaggery

4 eggs

2 egg yolks

1/4 teaspoon of ground cardomom

1/4 teaspoon of ground nutmeg

1/4 ground cloves

500ml of coconut milk

Pinch of salt

tools

14cm x 22cm x 4cm baking dish or a few baking molds as needed
