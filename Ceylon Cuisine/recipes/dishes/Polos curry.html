Polos Curry

This Sri Lankan Jackfruit Curry is packed with so much flavor, you won’t even realize it’s meatless! This curry is perfect for vegetarians, vegans, and gluten free diets, or anyone who loves curry for that matter!

prep time

1hr

cook time

1.5hr

serving

6 servings

Tags : Dishes

instructions

step 1

Dry roast the spice powders until golden brown. Keep it aside. Remember not to burn. Roast 1/2 tsp. of mustard seeds. Grind it into powder and keep aside.

step 2

Remove the skin of Polos. Cut into pieces/cubes.

step 3

Heat 2-3 tbsp. of oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds once the oil is hot. When mustard seeds are popping up, add onion, dried chili, green chili, garlic, Curry leaves, Pandan leaves (Rampe) into the mix. Also add cinnamon, cardamom & cloves. When onions are tender, add the Polos pieces into the pan & mix well. Add crushed black pepper too. Add thick coconut milk into the mix together with another 1 cup of water. Add Goroka or tamarind juice. Add about 1 tsp. salt. Mix well.

step 4

Cook polos curry until all pieces are soft and well cooked. Usually, it will take at least 30 minutes to cook Polos. Once it is cooked /boiled, simmer the polos curry in low flame for another 30 minutes until the gravy becomes thick and creamy. When it is done, polos pieces will turn into reddish brown color. The more you simmer this curry the more it tastes. Once the curry is ready, sprinkle the powdered mustard seeds that we kept aside at Step 1.

step 5

Serve with white rice or string hoppers.

ingredients

1 no of Polos (Baby Jackfruit)

1 medium sized polos is enough for about 5-6 servings

2-3 tbsp red chili powder

1 tsp turmeric powder

2-3 tbsp Sri Lankan Raw Curry Powder

1 medium sized onion slice

2-3 dried red chili

2-3 Green Chili

2 pieces of Goroka (gamboge) or (replace with 1 tomato or tamarind paste 1 tsp.)

3 cloves of garlic chopped.

1 tsp. crushed black pepper.

1’ piece cinnamon

2 cardamoms

2 cloves

1 tsp. mustard seeds

1 tsp fenugreek seeds

2-3 cups thick Coconut milk

salt to taste

Cooking oil

Curry leaves

Pandan leaves (Rampe)

tools

Clay Pot

Knife
