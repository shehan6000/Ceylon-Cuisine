Aluwa

Kokis are traditional Sri Lankan celebratory cookies served for many cultural events and celebrations, especially to celebrate Sinhala and Hindu New Year in April. This beautiful snack is crunchy, coconutty, and has a hint of sweetness.

prep time

6 min.

cook time

90 min.

serving

36 servings

Tags : Sweets

instructions

step 1

.Add the rice flour, all-purpose flour, salt, turmeric, and sugar to a large bowl and whisk to combine. Add the coconut milk and water a little at a time and whisk until a thick, smooth batter forms. Transfer a small portion of batter to a small, deep bowl where the Kokis mold can fit.

step 2

.Heat the coconut oil in a deep frying pan or wok over medium heat until you can insert a wooden chopstick into the oil and bubbles immediately start to form around it.

step 3

.Heat the kokis mold in the hot oil for 15â€“20 seconds, then gently shake the mold to remove excess oil. Dip the mold into the batter until the batter touches the upper rim of the mold, then immediately dip the mold with the batter into the hot oil. Fry with the mold for about 1 minute to set the shape, then lift and remove the mold and continue frying until golden yellow and crispy, about 45 seconds more. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate and repeat with the remaining batter.

step 4

.Let the kokis cool completely before serving. Leftover kokis will keep in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Enjoy!

ingredients

2 cups of rice flour

1/4 cup of all purpose flour

1/2 teaspoon of salt

1/2 teaspoon of ground turmeric

1 teaspoon of sugar

4 cups of full fat coconut milk

1/4 of a cup of water

Coconut, vegetable or canola oil for frying

tools

A kokis mold
